Jukely iOS

Company

Jukely

Role

Product Designer

Project

During the winter of 2014, Jukely went through a major product and business evolution. For years the company had been focused on building a free concert notification and discovery app, but that winter the team launched the first version of an entirely new paid product - a concert subscription service called Jukely Unlimited.

This new service, sandwiched into the existing product, gave subscribers access to an unlimited number of concerts from a curated list of shows happening in their city every month. Explosive new user growth drove the company to pivot to focusing on the feature almost exclusively. Unfortunately, poor product IA/navigation, a mountain of tech/design debt and a muddled brand and product narrative were hurting conversion of free users to paid subscribers.

As one of Jukely’s first product designers, I helped lead a focused redesigned of the iOS experience from concept to launch. My contributions involved high-level product strategy, ideation, rapid-prototyping, interaction design, qualitative and quantitative testing, visual design, product writing and software development in Swift. In December 2015 it was featured in Apple's Best New Apps.
